aloha editor • barcamp 2013

the Aloha Editor is an Open Source project built around the idea of creating a rich-text content creator for the open web.

Between June 6 and 7, the Aloha Editor community is hosting a barcamp to share some of the technologies and ideas around WYSIWYG editors, JavaScript, and HTML5, right in the heart of Vienna.

If you've ever had to write contentEditable="true" in any of your code or markup, then you really need be there!

Not to hype this too much, but... it's gonna be awesome!

A BarCamp emphasises human interaction.

The Aloha Editor BarCamp will be a place for professionals, hackers, and enthusiasts to share and explorer technology and ideas around WYSIWYG editors, and emerging web technology.

It's an opportunity to ask "why the heck...", and "how on earth do I..." questions to experts who can tell you the difference between nodeName, and tagName, and which combination of Internet Explorer's 16 browser/document modes will crash your code.

If you are working with WYSIWYG editors or with web technology in general, this barcamp will be a great opportunity to hone your development skills, learn best practices and concepts, and a chance to write code with great JavaScript developers.

Oh... and it's free, by the way!
